claude’s output : costoftrying business model (click to expand)

last updated: april 2026

what this is

costoftrying is a community-embedded film production studio. the through-line across everything is storytelling — using film to document, elevate, and connect creative communities.

it is not just a freelance filmmaking operation. it is not just a community page. the differentiator is that the production work comes from inside the community, and the community grows through the work.

the three layers

1. pjk1m — personal brand

  • documents the journey of building the craft and the company
  • attracts trust, attention, and collaborators
  • lives on social media and personal content channels
  • this is the entry point for most people

2. costoftrying films — production arm

  • produces films for and with people in the community
  • client work: event coverage, short docs, branded content for founders and creatives
  • original work: short films, personal projects, passion pieces
  • the output that builds the portfolio and the reputation

3. costoftrying community — the ecosystem

  • creative retreats, showcases, community events
  • partnerships with existing communities (midnight club, shift, v1, etc.)
  • low-friction client pipeline — work flows naturally from people already in the circle
  • exposure and credibility from showing up for others

how the flywheel works

personal brand (pjk1m)
    → builds credibility and audience
    → attracts collaborators and clients

community work
    → creates relationships and trust
    → feeds organic client pipeline to the production arm

films produced
    → become portfolio and proof of work
    → become content that markets the brand and community
    → loop back to growing all three layers

each layer feeds the other two. this is not three separate things — it is one thing expressed three ways.

business model in practice

how revenue gets generated:

  • video and film work for people inside communities (low sales friction, pre-existing trust)
  • event documentation for retreats, showcases, and community activations
  • short-form branded content for founders and creative projects
  • over time: original film projects that build the studio's creative reputation

why this works as a solo operation:

  • community relationships replace a sales funnel
  • content creation (personal brand) replaces traditional marketing
  • being embedded in the community means clients already know and trust the work before reaching out

the three goals

  1. build a community of creatives — keep showing up, keep hosting, keep collaborating
  2. get really good at film — make a lot of work, get technical, develop a distinct voice
  3. make it self-sustaining — let the flywheel run so the community funds the films and the films grow the community

what to lead with depending on context

room you're walking into how to frame costoftrying
potential film client production studio rooted in community
creative community the people behind the retreats and docs
someone who finds your content a filmmaker building something in public

same name. same vibe. different emphasis.

what not to change

  • the name — costoftrying has soul and the equity is already being built. don't start over.
  • the overlap between personal and brand — the merge is intentional. pjk1m and costoftrying reinforce each other, they don't compete.
  • the community focus — it is the moat. anyone can pick up a camera. not everyone is genuinely embedded in the communities they film.

the one sentence version

costoftrying is a film production studio that comes from the community — built by one filmmaker who believes storytelling and genuine connection are the same thing.
